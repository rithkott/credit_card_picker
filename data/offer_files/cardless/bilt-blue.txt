Product name: Bilt Blue Card (entry-level / no-annual-fee tier of "Bilt Card 2.0" three-card lineup)

Issuer: Column N.A., Member FDIC, pursuant to license from Mastercard International Incorporated. Cardless Inc. is the servicer of the Bilt Cards.

Launch: Bilt Card 2.0 lineup launched Feb 7, 2026, replacing the single Wells Fargo-issued Bilt Mastercard (Wells Fargo Bilt cardholders were auto-converted; legacy WF Bilt product effectively sunset — see Wells Fargo Autograph note in backlog).

Annual Fee
- $0 ("no annual fee")
- $0 annual fee for authorized users

Signup / Welcome Offer
- "$100 of Bilt Cash when you apply and get approved" (per Upgraded Points / newsroom summary)
- Newsroom phrasing: "$100 in Bilt Cash on account opening"

Earn Rates
- 1x points on everyday (non-housing) purchases
- 4% back in Bilt Cash on everyday spend (stacks with the 1x points — i.e., "1x points plus 4% back in Bilt Cash on everyday spending")
- Up to 1x points on rent and mortgage payments, paid through Bilt, with no transaction fee — earned via a separate credit line from everyday purchases (per Upgraded Points reporting on the mechanic; unlimited, capped at 1x)

Rent / Mortgage No-Fee Mechanic (central Bilt value prop, applies across all three cards)
- Rent and mortgage payments made through Bilt do not incur the transaction fee that would normally apply to paying rent by credit card.
- Housing payments run through a "separate credit line" from everyday spend (per Upgraded Points).
- Point-earning on housing payments is capped at up to 1x points and is funded by redeeming accumulated Bilt Cash: "Redeeming $3 of Bilt Cash unlocks 100 points on housing payments" (Upgraded Points quote). Example given: a $2,000 mortgage payment earning the full 1x points requires $60 of Bilt Cash, which in turn requires roughly $1,500 of everyday card spending (at the 4% Bilt Cash back rate).
- Support-article phrasing of the same mechanic: "For every $30 in Bilt Cash earned, you can unlock 1,000 Bilt Points when you make rent or mortgage payments." (Note: the $30/1,000-points and $3/100-points ratios from different sources are consistent — both express $0.03 of Bilt Cash per 1 point.)

Bilt Cash
- Earned at 4% back on everyday spend across all three cards.
- Expires annually on Dec 31, with a maximum of $100 of Bilt Cash allowed to roll into the next year (per Upgraded Points).
- Used both as a statement-credit-like reward and as the "fuel" to unlock housing-payment points (see mechanic above).
