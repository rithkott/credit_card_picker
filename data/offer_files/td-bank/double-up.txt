TD Double Up Credit Card (TD Bank)

Full product name per Cardholder Agreement: TD Double Up Visa® Credit Card ("TD Double Up").

Annual Fee
- None ($0).

Signup Bonus (One-Time Bonus Offer)
- 20,000 Bonus Points, equal to a $200 statement credit, if you are approved for an Account in response to this specific offer.
- To qualify: spend $1,500 in total Purchases using the Account within 90 days of the Account opening date.
- Provided the Account is open and in good standing, the 20,000 Bonus Points ($200 statement credit) will be reflected on the Account within 6 to 8 weeks after you have met the eligibility criteria.
- This One-Time Bonus Offer is not available if you open an account in response to a different offer you may receive from TD Bank, or if you previously received a One-Time Bonus Offer on this account or any other account with TD Bank.

Earn Rates / "Double Up" Cash Back Mechanics
- You earn 2 Points for each $1 of eligible Purchases (less credits, returns and adjustments), each billing cycle. This is the source of the "Double Up" name — points accrue at 2 Points per dollar, not 1.
- Marketing description: "Earn unlimited 2% Cash Back on all eligible purchases – no rotating Spend Categories, no caps or limits," provided the Account is open and in good standing.
- Points are earned on each individual Purchase transaction; TD rounds up to the next whole Point if the calculation results in 1/2 of a Point or more, and rounds down if less than 1/2 of a Point.
- Points earned are estimated based on the authorization amount reported by retailers and are subject to change based on returns and retailer adjustments.
- You may be offered opportunities to earn additional Points ("Bonus Points"); related terms are provided with the specific offer.
- Transactions that do NOT earn Points (not eligible for Purchase Redemption Credits): Balance Transfers; Cash Advances (including convenience checks and transactions to obtain travelers checks, money orders, lottery tickets, casino gaming chips, wire transfers, foreign currency, cryptocurrency, debt repayments, race track wagers, legal online wagers or similar betting transactions, prepaid cards, or any other similar cash-like transactions); fees; interest charges; disputed and unauthorized or fraudulent charges; account refunds, rebates, and similar credits posted to the account.

How Points Convert to Cash Back (the "second half" of the double-up mechanic)
- You may redeem once you have accumulated at least 2,500 Points, as either a statement credit or direct deposit into a TD Bank checking or savings account located in the United States ("Cash Back").
- Upon redemption, 1 Point = $0.01 (one penny) when redeemed for Cash Back. Example given in the Rewards Agreement: 20,000 Points are redeemable for $200 Cash Back.
- Net effect: 2 Points/$1 earned x $0.01/Point redemption value = 2% cash back on eligible purchases.
- Account statement credits will reduce the outstanding balance, but the cardholder is still required to make at least the minimum monthly payment.
- Points do not expire and there is no limit to the number of Points that may be earned, as long as the Account remains open and in good standing.

Redemption
- Points may only be redeemed by the primary Cardholder, at specified levels, for Cash Back, travel, and other redemption options TD may offer, including "Pay with Points" at participating merchants ("Participating Parties"). Specific travel redemption options are subject to availability and may carry additional terms from merchants/service providers/third parties/TD.
- Cardholders may be required to self-register, or may be registered automatically, with a Participating Party to use Pay with Points; TD may share Account information with that party.
- Each Point is worth at least $0.01 when redeemed for Cash Back, Pay with Points, or travel; other redemption options may be worth more, less, or have no assigned dollar value. Limited-time promotional offers may be available.
- TD does not have to offer all redemption options and can cancel or change them at any time.
- Redeem via the Rewards Program Website (log in to tdbank.com or the TD Bank app, select Credit Card Account, then "Rewards") or by calling 1-888-561-8861.
- Points may be forfeited immediately if the Account is closed due to: serious delinquency (TD determines what constitutes "serious delinquency," but never less than 60 days), or fraudulent activity/misuse associated with the Account or Rewards Program.
- If the Account is closed for any other reason (by cardholder or by TD) or is not/will not be open to new transactions, unredeemed Points are forfeited if not redeemed within 100 calendar days of Account closure (or the date in any forfeiture notice).
- Points balance is reduced for returns, credits, and adjustments. TD may disqualify customers from earning/redeeming Points, or close the Account, for fraud, program abuse, or violation of the Rewards Agreement.
- Points have no cash value until redeemed, are not the cardholder's property, and may not be transferred or assigned.
- TD may change, restrict, modify, cancel, or terminate the Rewards Program at any time; material changes (e.g., reducing the Point earn rate, or raising the minimum Cash Back redemption amount) come with advance notice as required by law. Other changes may be posted to the Rewards Program Website without prior notice.

APR / Interest
- Purchase APR: 18.49%, 23.49%, or 28.49% variable, based on creditworthiness. All APRs vary with the market based on the Prime Rate.
- Balance Transfer APR: 0% introductory APR for the first 15 billing cycles, for balance transfers made within the first 90 days of account opening; after that, 18.49%, 23.49%, or 28.49% variable, based on creditworthiness.
- Cash Advance APR: 28.74% variable.
- Margins added to the Prime Rate Index: 11.74%, 16.74%, or 21.74% for Purchases and Balance Transfers; 21.99% for Cash Advances. (Index = Prime Rate (U.S.) as published in The Wall Street Journal; Prime Rate was 6.75% as of December 11, 2025.)
- Due date is at least 25 days after the close of each billing cycle; no interest on purchases if entire balance is paid by the due date each month. Interest on Balance Transfers and Cash Advances begins accruing as of the transaction date.
- Minimum Interest Charge: no less than $1.00 if charged.
- Balance calculation method: "Average Daily Balance (including Current Transactions)."

Fees
- Annual Fee: None.
- Balance Transfer fee: intro fee of $5 or 3% of the transfer amount (whichever is greater) on transfers made within the first 90 days of account opening; after that, $5 or 5% of the transfer amount (whichever is greater).
- Cash Advance fee: $10 or 5% of the cash advance amount, whichever is greater.
- Foreign Transaction fee: 3% of each transaction, in U.S. dollars.
- Late Payment fee: up to $40.
- Returned Payment fee: up to $40.
- Minimum credit line: $500 or more, based on TD's evaluation of the applicant's credit report and other factors.

Balance Transfer Details
- Balance Transfers are subject to a fee (see above) and to available credit limit.
- The Balance Transfer transaction fee is added to the minimum monthly payment amount in the month the transfer posts.
- TD may limit the number/types of accounts from which Balance Transfers are allowed, and the times/manner/circumstances in which they may be requested.
- Balance Transfers may not be made payable to yourself or to cash; may be used to pay off other creditors but not accounts owned by TD Bank or any bank currently/previously affiliated with TD Bank.
- Processing takes 2 to 4 weeks after account opening; continue paying other creditors until the transfer appears as a credit on that creditor's statement. Other credit card accounts are not automatically closed by a balance transfer.
- To keep the 0% introductory/promotional balance-transfer APR on new purchases interest-free, each month by the due date the cardholder must pay: (1) Minimum Payment Due; plus (2) total outstanding purchase balance; plus (3) total outstanding cash advance balance; plus (4) total outstanding balance-transfer balances not subject to the 0% intro/promo APR; plus (5) any fees assessed to the Account.
- Convenience Checks are processed as Cash Advances, not Balance Transfers. Paying a disputed creditor via Balance Transfer or Convenience Check may forfeit certain dispute rights with that creditor.
