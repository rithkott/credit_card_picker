Luxury Card Mastercard family (Gold / Black / Titanium)

Shared program facts (all three tiers)
- All three cards are metal cards issued under the Luxury Card brand, each a Mastercard World Elite Mastercard product.
- Rewards program (where offered) runs through LuxuryCardTravel.com: 2 points/$1 on airfare and hotel reservations booked through LuxuryCardTravel.com; 1 point/$1 on all other purchases. Redemption: ~2% value toward airfare, ~1%-1.5% value for cash back (statement credit); sources conflict on the exact cash-back rate for Black Card (see Gaps below). No stated blackout dates or point expiration while account is open (per aggregator summaries; not independently confirmed on an official terms page).
- Complimentary benefits averaging $750 per stay at 3,000+ luxury properties worldwide when booking hotels through LuxuryCardTravel.com (room upgrades when available, complimentary Wi-Fi, breakfast for two, and other amenities), on top of the 2X points on that booking.
- 24/7 Luxury Card Concierge service across all three tiers: personalized travel planning, event/ticket access, gifting, and VIP experience booking.
- 0% foreign transaction fee stated for Titanium and Gold Cards; reported as 0% for Black Card as well by aggregators, but not independently confirmed on an official Black Card terms page (see Gaps).
- Mastercard Global Service emergency card replacement is referenced as a benefit; explicit replacement-card fee amount (if any) was not found on official pages (see Gaps).
- Each tier explicitly allows Authorized Users at a separate annual fee per AU (amount differs by tier, see per-tier sections).

=== Mastercard Titanium Card ===
Annual Fee
- $299 annual fee ($149 for each Authorized User).

Earn Rates / Rewards
- 2 points/$1 on airfare and hotel bookings made through LuxuryCardTravel.com.
- 1 point/$1 on all other purchases.
- Redemption: ~2% value toward airfare; ~1% value for cash back.
- No stated sign-up bonus.

Perks
- Complimentary Priority Pass Select membership: access to 1,700+ airport lounges worldwide.
- Complimentary benefits averaging $750/stay at 3,000+ luxury properties booked via LuxuryCardTravel.com (in addition to 2X points on that booking).
- 24/7 Luxury Card Concierge service (travel planning, exclusive tickets, gifting, VIP experiences).
- Cell phone protection: up to $1,000/year for eligible claims (activated by paying your monthly mobile bill with the card; covers repair/replacement cost if phone is damaged or stolen).
- 0% foreign transaction fee.
- Mastercard Global Service emergency card replacement referenced as included; no dollar fee found for standard/expedited replacement.

=== Mastercard Gold Card ===
Annual Fee
- $1,199 annual fee ($349 for each Authorized User).
- Card is plated in 24K Gold, ~22 grams, metal construction.

Earn Rates / Rewards
- 2 points/$1 on airfare and hotel bookings made through LuxuryCardTravel.com.
- 1 point/$1 on all other purchases.
- Same double-points structure as Titanium; the Gold Card's premium over Titanium is driven by its statement-credit package (below), not a materially different earn rate.

Perks / Annual Statement Credits
- Up to $300/year in statement credits toward flight-related purchases (airline tickets, baggage fees, upgrades, and more).
- Up to $200/year in statement credits at qualified restaurants.
- Up to $200/year in "Ship&Play" credits (for sending belongings ahead).
- Up to $120 reimbursement for a Global Entry or TSA PreCheck application fee.
- Complimentary benefits averaging $750/stay at 3,000+ luxury properties booked via LuxuryCardTravel.com.
- 24/7 Luxury Card Concierge service.
- 0% foreign transaction fee.
- 0% introductory APR for 15 months on balance transfers made within 45 days of account opening (regular APR 19.49%-27.49% variable, based on creditworthiness, per aggregator reporting — not independently confirmed on an official Schumer box); 5% balance transfer fee reported by aggregators.
- Note: unlike Titanium, no Priority Pass / lounge-access benefit was found advertised for Gold Card in the sources reviewed (see Gaps — confirm before relying on this).

=== Mastercard Black Card ===
Annual Fee
- $699 annual fee. Authorized User fee reported inconsistently across sources as $195/year and $249/year — could not confirm the current correct figure (see Gaps). A separate, apparently outdated promotional landing page (luxurycard.com/landing/2022-001) references a "$495 Annual Fee ($195 for each Authorized User)" offer, suggesting the fee has changed over time and/or varies by offer/vintage.
- Card is 22-gram stainless-steel-and-carbon metal construction.

Earn Rates / Rewards
- 2 points/$1 on airfare and hotel bookings made through LuxuryCardTravel.com.
- 1 point/$1 on all other purchases.
- Redemption: ~2% value toward airfare. Cash-back redemption value reported inconsistently across sources as 1% and 1.5% — could not confirm which is current (see Gaps).

Perks / Annual Statement Credits
- Up to $200/year in statement credits toward flight-related purchases (airline tickets, baggage fees, upgrades, and more).
- Up to $100/year in statement credits at qualified restaurants worldwide.
- Up to $120 reimbursement for a Global Entry or TSA PreCheck application fee.
- Complimentary Priority Pass Select: airport lounge access for cardholder and guests.
- Complimentary benefits averaging $750/stay at 3,000+ luxury properties booked via LuxuryCardTravel.com.
- 24/7 Luxury Card Concierge service.
- Cell phone protection: up to $1,000/year for eligible claims (pay monthly mobile bill with the card to activate).
- Trip cancellation/interruption insurance: reimbursement of non-refundable round-trip expenses up to $5,000/insured person for covered cancellation/interruption reasons.
- Baggage delay insurance: up to $100/day (max 3 qualifying days) for baggage delays of more than 4 hours from arrival at destination (arrival at primary residence excluded).
- Auto rental collision damage waiver; travel accident insurance (referenced by aggregators, specific coverage limits not found on an official page).
- 0% foreign transaction fee (reported by aggregators; not independently confirmed on an official Black Card terms page).
